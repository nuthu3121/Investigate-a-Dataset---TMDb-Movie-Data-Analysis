Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

In this project, we will be analyzing the data associated with user rating and revenue. In particular, we will focus on finding trends and insights about how popularity, genre and vote average influence the revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars, accounting for the inflation over time. The data contained information about 10,000 movies collected from the Movie Database (TMDb). The data contained 21 variables, which were: id, imdb_id, popularity, budget, revenue, original_title, cast, homepage, director, tagline, keywords, overview, runtime, genres, production_companies, release_date, vote_count, vote_average, release_year, budget_adj, and revenue_adj. The address the main aim of this project, I selected adjusted revenue as the dependent variable because it showed the actual revenue of the associated movie when the inflation over time has been accounted for. In addition, the independent variables choosen to address the main aim were, runtime, popularity, and average vote rating. Thus, in this project, we aimed to answer the folllowing research questions.

Questions for Analysis¶

What levels of average vote ratings yield the highest average adjusted revenue?

Do movies with high popularity yield higher adjusted revenue?

Do higher runtime movies yield higher adjusted revenues?

#define a function to calculate the bin edges,provide labels for vote rating levels and create a new categorical column
# named vote_average_levels
def cut_by_num_summary (df_name, col_name):
    #find min, max, and quartiles
    min_val =df_name[col_name].describe()[3]
    lower_quartile=df_name[col_name].describe()[4]
    medium_val = df_name[col_name].describe()[5]
    upper_quartile = df_name[col_name].describe()[6]
    max_val = df_name[col_name].describe()[7]
    # Bin edges that will be used to "cut" the data into groups
    bin_edges = [min_val,lower_quartile,medium_val,upper_quartile, max_val] 
    # Labels for the four average vote rating level groups
    bin_names = ['Low','Medium' ,'Moderately High' ,'High']
    #create the levels column
    name = '{}_levels'.format(col_name)
    df_name[name] = pd.cut(df_name[col_name], bin_edges, labels=bin_names,include_lowest = True)
    return df_name

To avoid repetitive codes, we created a function cut_by_num_summary (), which takes two arguments (df_name,and col_name), divide the specified columns into four groups using the five number summary, adds the new column to our dataframe and then returns the new data frame with added columns

Conclusions¶

From the findings, it is clear that higher popular movies tend to yield higher adjusted revenue. In addition, movies with higher runtime tend to yield relatively higher revenue than those with lower runtime. Additionally, the findings revealed that movies with higher votes on average are more likely to yield more adjusted revenue as opposed to those with lower votes. These findings indicates that movies companies should focus on creating movies with higher runtime, increase the quality of the movies in order to increase the average movie rating and also, to focus on advertisement. Following these strategies can increase the overall revenues after adjusting for inflation. However, this study only focused on three independent variables among many other potential variables that influence the adjusted revenue. As such, further research should focus on other factors such as genres, year of release, and other variables as they can also influence the amount of revenue generated from movies.
